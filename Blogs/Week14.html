Week 14

In my artistic and programming practice, I approach justice and ethics by centering accessibility, inclusion, and care at every stage of development. My website is not just a portfolio; it is a digital environment that reflects my values. That means considering who can access it, how they navigate it, and who might feel excluded by its design. This approach aligns with Sasha Costanza-Chock’s concept of design justice, which argues that design should center those who are often marginalized by default systems, rather than reinforcing dominant norms. I work to ensure that no part of the experience assumes a single way of seeing or knowing

This includes building features like alt text, responsive layouts for mobile users (which make up the majority in the context of South Africa) [which i still need to refine], and interactive elements that invite exploration rather than gatekeep information. Ethically, I avoid exploiting user data and aim to create a space where users are safe from unethical web design practices, seen, and empowered. Justice to me is not a feature but a foundation from which I build.
