For the reading, I chose “The Library of Babel” by Jorge Luis Borges (1941). I find it fascinating how Borges essentially conceptualized the internet long before its arrival, so much so that he was able to theorize problems that currently affect us within the existence of the internet. His depiction of an infinite library filled with every possible combination of letters mirrors the sheer amount of information available online today. The challenges of information overload resonate deeply with my current experience of the internet. With so much access to information and each other, it often feels overwhelming to navigate through just how much is out there. Borges discusses the difficulty and pointlessness in searching for meaning in such a chaotic environment, where individuals are bombarded with texts that may be irrelevant or contradictory. This resonates with my own struggles as I am fed countless texts, phots, videos, and social media posts, that often feels so overwhelming that it makes it difficult to willingly engage with the information, almost as though the abundance of information is somewhat uninviting. The library's inhabitants, in their quest for knowledge, remind me of how we seek validation through likes and shares, sometimes losing sight of the genuine pursuit of truth. That in this abundance of knowledge, there is just so much information that we have much access to that we ourselves know less. Moreover, Borges' explanation that no two books are identical yet many are similar resonates with the repetitive nature of online content, where trends and ideas frequently resurface without any new insights. This repetitive nature of information can lead to a sense of redundancy, making it challenging to find unique perspectives and information.Borges's work serves as commentary on the complexities of our digital age, where we are consistently overloaded with information, highlighting the paradox of seeking knowledge in an infinite and chaotic landscape, a struggle that continues to shape my experience on the internet today.
